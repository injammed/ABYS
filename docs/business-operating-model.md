# ABYS Business Operating Model

ABYS is not complete as a concept repository. ABYS is complete only when it participates in financial circulation.

## Operating thesis

ABYS converts painful, boring, high-value operational problems into paid automation offers. The business exists to move from intent to money to fulfilled outcome with as little interpretive drag as possible.

The canonical circulation loop is:

```txt
lead -> qualified problem -> paid offer -> delivered outcome -> invoice/payment -> support/retention -> case study/data -> improved offer -> repeatable pipeline
```

## Full-mode business surfaces

Every ABYS business artifact must strengthen at least one surface:

1. Customer segment
2. Painful operational problem
3. Paid offer
4. Landing page or outbound script
5. Intake form
6. Qualification score
7. Pricing model
8. Payment or invoice state
9. Fulfillment workflow
10. Delivery artifact
11. Retention loop
12. Dashboard metric
13. Legal/accounting checklist
14. Repo issue, code, test, workflow, or deployment

Material that does not strengthen one of these surfaces is not business material. It should be redirected to ITEM, redirected to SYNTEL, refined, deprecated, or deleted.

## Initial paid offer

The first ABYS offer should be a boring-problem automation service:

**We turn messy operational documents into structured workflows, files, dashboards, and automations.**

Initial wedge examples:

- PDF invoice and purchase-order ingestion into spreadsheet or ERP-ready CSV
- prior authorization/admin packet preparation with human review
- vendor compliance evidence tracker
- reverse logistics returns processing dashboard
- cross-border document reconciliation assistant with human review

## Delivery rule

A sold ABYS engagement must terminate in at least one concrete delivery surface:

- cleaned dataset
- generated report
- working script
- dashboard
- workflow automation
- validated task packet
- integration checklist
- operational SOP
- measurable cycle-time or error-rate improvement

## Business canon-vs-slop rule

Preserve:

- buyer-specific pain
- offer language a stranger understands in under 30 seconds
- pricing and scope boundaries
- intake and qualification data
- payment status
- reusable delivery workflows
- tests and validation
- post-delivery metrics

Reject or quarantine:

- concepts with no buyer
- concepts with no payment path
- concepts with no delivery workflow
- vague platform ambition without wedge offer
- mythic language that does not improve trust, conversion, retention, or pricing power
- architecture that cannot be shipped by a solo founder or small agent stack

## Minimum viable circulation

The first functional ABYS business loop is not a huge platform. It is:

1. One landing-page promise
2. One intake form
3. One qualification score
4. One fixed-scope offer
5. One invoice/payment status field
6. One delivery checklist
7. One retention follow-up
8. One case-study template

When this loop exists, ABYS begins operating as infrastructure inside financial streams.
